Make a room, send the code to one person. You each pick your own language — the conversation reaches each of you in your own words.

Experimental languages may translate poorly — only use them if you must.

🔒 What stays private: messages are scrambled on your phone before they're sent — Ngumzo's relay never sees your words. Translation and "hear it" voice are done by Google, so the text of a message you translate or play aloud is sent to Google. Anyone with the room code can join — share it carefully.
